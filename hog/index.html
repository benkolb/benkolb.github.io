I am a PhD Candidate in Economics with a focus on monetary and hog economics. My research focuses on unconventional monetary policy measures, hogs and bacon, and their effect on the business cycle.

Abstract of my Hog Market Paper (HMP): In this paper, I address the effects of shocks originating in the bacon sector, introduced in an add-hog fashion into an otherwise standard DSGE model. To the best of my knowledge, this is the first paper to put bacon frictions into a DSGE model. Shocks originating in that sector, labelled hog shocks, explain up to 80% of US GDP volatility. This somewhat surprisingly large number can be convincingly explained by the fact that sausage shocks capture the dynamics of a quintessential Aggregate Demand shock. Interesting policy prescriptions arise: In particular, an Optimum Quantity of Bacon is found to be welfare-improving.
